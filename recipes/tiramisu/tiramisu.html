Tiramisu is a classic Italian dessert made of layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers and a rich, creamy mascarpone cheese mixture. Its delightful flavor and smooth texture make it a favorite among dessert lovers!

Tiramisu

Fun Fact about Tiramisu

Did you know that Tiramisu means "pick me up" in Italian? This dessert is not only delicious but also gives you a little boost from the espresso!

Ingredients

  • 6 egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 1 1/4 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup mascarpone cheese
  • 1 cup brewed espresso, cooled
  • 24 ladyfingers
  • Cocoa powder (for dusting)
  • Dark chocolate shavings (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. In a saucepan, whisk together egg yolks and sugar over medium heat until the mixture is thick and pale.
  2. Gradually whisk in milk and cook until it thickens, about 5 minutes. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
  3. In a mixing bowl, beat the heavy cream until stiff peaks form. Fold in the mascarpone cheese until smooth.
  4. Combine the cooled egg mixture with the mascarpone mixture, folding gently to combine.
  5. Dip each ladyfinger briefly into the cooled espresso, making sure not to soak them.
  6. Layer half of the dipped ladyfingers in the bottom of a 9x13 inch dish.
  7. Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers.
  8. Add another layer of dipped ladyfingers, followed by the remaining mascarpone mixture.
  9. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight to allow the flavors to meld.
  10. Before serving, dust the top with cocoa powder and garnish with dark chocolate shavings if desired.
  11. Slice and serve chilled. Enjoy your homemade Tiramisu!
