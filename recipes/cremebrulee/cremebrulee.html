Crème Brûlée is a classic French dessert that features a rich and creamy custard base topped with a layer of hard caramel. Its contrasting textures and delicate flavor make it a beloved treat for any occasion!

Crème Brûlée

Fun Fact about Crème Brûlée

Did you know that Crème Brûlée means "burnt cream" in French? The dish originated in France and has been enjoyed since the 17th century. Its signature caramelized sugar top is achieved by using a kitchen torch or broiler, creating a satisfying crack when you tap it with a spoon!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup additional granulated sugar (for caramelizing)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C).
  2. In a saucepan, heat the heavy cream and vanilla extract over medium heat until it just begins to simmer. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
  3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and 1/2 cup of granulated sugar until the mixture is thick and pale.
  4. Gradually pour the warm cream mixture into the egg yolk mixture while whisking continuously to avoid curdling the eggs.
  5. Strain the custard mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a measuring cup or bowl to remove any lumps.
  6. Divide the custard among ramekins, filling each about 3/4 full.
  7. Place the ramekins in a baking dish and carefully fill the dish with hot water until it reaches halfway up the sides of the ramekins.
  8. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-40 minutes, or until the edges are set but the centers are still slightly jiggly.
  9. Remove the ramekins from the water bath and allow them to cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
  10. Before serving, sprinkle a thin, even layer of granulated sugar on top of each custard. Use a kitchen torch to caramelize the sugar until golden brown, or place under the broiler for 1-2 minutes, watching closely to avoid burning.
  11. Let the Crème Brûlée sit for a few minutes before serving to allow the caramelized sugar to harden. Enjoy!
